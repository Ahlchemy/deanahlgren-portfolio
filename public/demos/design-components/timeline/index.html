Timeline Component

This component displays events in chronological order with alternating left and right positioning.

1876

Telephone Invented

Alexander Graham Bell patents the first practical telephone, revolutionizing long-distance communication and launching a new era of connectivity.

1903

First Powered Flight

The Wright brothers achieve the first powered, sustained, and controlled airplane flight at Kitty Hawk, North Carolina.

1947

Invention of the Transistor

John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley invent the transistor at Bell Labs, laying the groundwork for modern electronics and the Information Age.

1969

Moon Landing

Neil Armstrong becomes the first person to walk on the moon during NASA's Apollo 11 mission, declaring it "one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."

1989

World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee invents the World Wide Web while working at CERN, creating the foundation for the modern internet as we know it today.
