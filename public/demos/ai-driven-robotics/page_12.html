Transfer Learning: From Simulation to Reality

Transfer learning in robotics bridges the gap between simulated environments and real-world deployment, enabling robots to apply knowledge across different tasks and scenarios.

Domain Randomization

Training in randomized simulated environments to make policies robust against variations. Creates resilience to differences between simulation and reality.

Improves real-world performance

Meta-Learning

Algorithms like MAML (Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning) enable robots to quickly adapt to new tasks with minimal data through "learning to learn".

Fast adaptation to new scenarios

Curriculum Learning

Gradually increasing task complexity during training, allowing robots to develop more generalizable skills that transfer well to new scenarios.

Builds transferable skill foundations

Sim-to-Real Transfer Process

1

Train policy in simulation with domain randomization

2

Apply domain adaptation techniques to bridge reality gap

3

Use progressive neural networks to prevent forgetting

4

Fine-tune with minimal real-world data for optimal performance
