Hands-On Challenge: Sim-to-Real Transfer

Domain randomization is a powerful technique that helps bridge the reality gap when transferring policies learned in simulation to real robots. Follow these steps to create a robust grasping policy:

1

Setup Simulation

  • Create a basic grasping simulation with a robotic arm and target objects
  • Define the state space (joint angles, object position) and action space (gripper movements)
2

Apply Randomization

  • Randomize lighting conditions, textures, and camera positions
  • Vary physical properties: friction, mass, object dimensions
  • Introduce random noise to sensor readings
3

Deploy & Evaluate

  • Transfer learned policy directly to the real robot
  • Measure success rate of grasping attempts
  • Fine-tune with minimal real-world data if needed
Increasing Robustness

Your Challenge

Design a domain randomization strategy for a robotic grasping task that addresses at least 5 different environmental variables. Consider which randomizations would have the most impact on successful real-world transfer.

Tip: Focus on randomizing aspects that are most likely to differ between simulation and reality, such as lighting, friction coefficients, and sensor noise.
