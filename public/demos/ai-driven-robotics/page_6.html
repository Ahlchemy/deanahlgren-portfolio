Real-World Challenges in Robotic RL

Sample Efficiency

Real-world interactions with robots are costly and time-consuming

Solution: Learning from as few samples as possible through model-based approaches

Sim-to-Real Transfer

Policies learned in simulation often perform poorly when transferred to real robots

Solution: Domain randomization to create more robust policies

Continuous & High-Dimensional Action Spaces

Many robotic tasks involve continuous control of multiple joints, creating complex action spaces

Solution: Hierarchical RL for handling complex tasks

Partial Observability

Robots often have incomplete information about their environment, making state determination difficult

Solution: Memory-based policies and belief state estimation

Safety Constraints

Ensuring safe exploration and operation of robots during the learning process

Solution: Safe RL methods to ensure constraint satisfaction during learning

Addressing These Challenges

  • Domain randomization for sim-to-real transfer
  • Hierarchical RL for complex tasks
  • Safe RL methods for constraint satisfaction
  • Improved sample efficiency techniques
