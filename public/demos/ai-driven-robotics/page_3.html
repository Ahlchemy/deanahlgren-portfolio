Learning Through Interaction: The RL Process

Key Insight

Reinforcement learning enables robots to make decisions by interacting with their environment and receiving feedback, without requiring explicit programming for every scenario.

1. Agent (Robot)

Selects an action based on current policy

2. Environment

Changes state based on robot's action

3. Reward

Feedback signal based on action outcome

4. Learning

Updates policy to maximize future rewards

The Trial and Error Process

Through repeated cycles of interaction, robots gradually optimize their decision-making strategies. For example, a robotic arm learns optimal grasping by attempting different approaches and receiving positive rewards for successful grasps, improving its policy with each iteration.
