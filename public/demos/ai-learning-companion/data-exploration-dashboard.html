Understanding Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Exploratory Data Analysis is the critical first step in any data science project. Through interactive visualizations, we can:

  • Identify patterns: See relationships between variables (e.g., income vs. miles)
  • Spot outliers: Find unusual data points that might need special attention
  • Understand distributions: See how data is spread across different values
  • Guide feature engineering: Decide which variables to use in machine learning models

Use the filters above to explore different segments of the data and see how patterns change!

Business Application: Customer Segmentation

Scenario: CardioGood Fitness wants to understand their customer base to improve marketing and product development:

  • Age Distribution: Helps plan age-targeted marketing campaigns
  • Income vs Usage: Identifies high-value customer segments
  • Product Preferences: Guides inventory and product development decisions
  • Fitness Levels: Informs product positioning and feature development

This type of analysis directly informs business strategy and helps companies make data-driven decisions!
