# Content Analysis for Interactive E-Learning Course

## Document 1: Beyond the Numbers - Discovering the Fascinating History of Data Science

### Key Learning Objectives:
- Describe the historical context of decision-making
- Illustrate and differentiate between inferential and computational paradigms of data science
- Explore the evolution of inferential and computational paradigms over the years through key innovations
- Interpret the impact of these innovations on further advancements and developments

### Main Topics Covered:
1. **Origin of Decisions**
   - Historical context: Decisions were always data-driven
   - Early civilization examples: firewood stocking, travel timing, crop cultivation
   - Connection to modern business decision-making
   - Evolution of statistical techniques and computing capabilities

2. **Paradigms in Data Science**
   - **Inferential Paradigm:**
     - Focus: Make predictions on population based on sample data
     - Methods: Use statistical methods to draw conclusions/inferences from data
     - Limitations: Representativeness of data
     - Examples: Medication effectiveness trials, policy impact assessment
   
   - **Computational Paradigm:**
     - Focus: Leverage computational methods and technology to scale insight generation
     - Methods: Implement algorithms and computational methods to analyze data
     - Limitations: Complexity of algorithms and cost of training large models
     - Examples: Weather forecasting, vehicle routing optimization

3. **Evolution of Data Science (1940s to present)**
   - Historical timeline and key innovations
   - Drivers of advancement
   - Impact on further developments

## Document 2: Introduction to Generative AI

### Key Learning Objectives:
- Define AI Fundamentals and Generative Models
- Deconstruct Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Analyze LLM behavior, understanding, completion, and prediction
- Investigate hallucinations in LLMs and underlying causes
- Identify diverse applications of ML, DL, and Generative AI across sectors
- Gain practical experience with ChatGPT for text and code generation

### Main Topics Covered:
1. **AI Fundamentals**
   - Defining AI, ML, DL, LLM and Generative Models via Venn diagram
   - Supervised and unsupervised ML tasks
   - Understanding Generative and Discriminative AI

2. **Generative AI Timeline and Models**
   - Brief timeline of Generative AI development
   - Overview of generative models
   - Technical foundations

3. **Large Language Models (LLMs)**
   - Deconstructing LLM behavior
   - Understanding text completion and prediction
   - Hallucination phenomenon and causes

4. **Applications and Practical Use**
   - ML, DL and GenAI applications in business, retail, health, and technology
   - Hands-on demonstration of ChatGPT for text and code generation
